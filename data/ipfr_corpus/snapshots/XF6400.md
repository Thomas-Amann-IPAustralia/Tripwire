Description:
These Communicable Diseases Network Australia (CDNA) guidelines aim to help public health units deal with outbreaks of norovirus and suspected viral gastroenteritis.
They capture the knowledge of experienced professionals, build on past research efforts, and provide advice on best practice based on the best available evidence at the time of completion.
They cover:
- surveillance and reporting
- trends
- lab diagnosis
- outbreaks and case definitions
- outbreak investigations
- infection control.
